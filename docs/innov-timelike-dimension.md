# Innovation debate 3 — what if an extra dimension is timelike?

## Heresy
The spacelike-ness of extra dimensions is an unexamined assumption.

## Evidence (simulations/round_innov-timelike.py)
- Symbolically derived (not recited): KK masses M_n^2 = -(n/R)^2 on a timelike circle;
  the A_y kinetic term flips sign (ghost) exactly for the timelike case
- Tachyon count below KK level N is exactly 2N: **20 / 200 / 2000** at N = 10/100/1000 —
  diverges with the cutoff; classical growth rates |M_n| = sqrt((n/R)^2 - m^2) unbounded
- Plot: simulations/outputs/innov_timelike_dimension.png

## Frontier map
1. **Why the heresy died (naive form):** infinitely many tachyons with unbounded growth
   rates plus a ghost — no bounded Hamiltonian, no unitarity.
2. **What saves the orthodoxy:** exactly those two requirements. Give them up or gauge them
   away and you reach the KNOWN escape: Bars' 2T-physics, where an Sp(2,R) gauge symmetry
   thins (d,2) signature back to effectively one time
   ([arXiv:hep-th/9703060](https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-th/9703060),
   [arXiv:hep-th/0106021](https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-th/0106021)).
3. **Frontier:** whether any constrained multi-time framework yields predictions that differ
   from 1T physics (so far the gauge-fixing returns standard physics).

Strong evidence against the naive version, not a proof against constrained frameworks.
Mathematical result only; experimental validation required for any claim about reality.
